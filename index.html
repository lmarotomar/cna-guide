General Questions · Preguntas Generales
1
Tell me about yourself.
Háblame sobre ti / Cuéntame de ti.
▼
🇺🇸 English

Keep it professional: mention your caring nature, any experience helping others — family caregiving, volunteering — and your motivation. Example: "I am a compassionate and reliable person. I cared for a family member and that inspired me to pursue a career where I can make a real difference every day."

🇪🇸 Español

Mantenlo profesional. Ej: "Soy una persona compasiva y responsable. Cuidé a un familiar y eso me inspiró a seguir una carrera donde puedo hacer una diferencia real cada día."

💡 TIP
Focus on empathy and reliability — avoid unrelated personal details.
2
Why do you want to work as a CNA/HHA?
¿Por qué deseas trabajar como CNA/HHA?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I want to make a meaningful difference in people lives every day. Caring for others — especially the elderly or those recovering — gives me a deep sense of purpose and fulfillment."

🇪🇸 Español

"Quiero hacer una diferencia significativa en la vida de las personas cada día. Cuidar a otros me da un profundo sentido de propósito y satisfacción."

💡 TIP
Be sincere. Employers value people who are genuinely motivated to serve.
3
What are your greatest strengths?
¿Cuáles son tus mayores fortalezas?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"My greatest strengths are my patience, my reliability, and my genuine compassion for every person I care for. I also communicate well with both patients and the care team."

🇪🇸 Español

"Mis mayores fortalezas son mi paciencia, mi responsabilidad y mi compasión genuina. También me comunico bien con los pacientes y el equipo de cuidado."

💡 TIP
Always connect your strengths to how they benefit the patient or team.
4
What is your greatest weakness?
¿Cuál es tu mayor debilidad?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I sometimes take on too much because I care deeply about doing everything right. I am learning to communicate better with my team and delegate when needed."

🇪🇸 Español

"A veces asumo demasiado porque me preocupo mucho por hacerlo todo bien. Estoy aprendiendo a delegar y comunicarme mejor con mi equipo."

💡 TIP
Never say you have no weaknesses. Self-awareness shows maturity.
5
How do you handle stress on the job?
¿Cómo manejas el estrés en el trabajo?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I prioritize my tasks and stay organized. When things feel overwhelming, I take a moment to breathe and refocus. I also communicate openly with my team — caregiving is truly a team effort."

🇪🇸 Español

"Priorizo mis tareas y me mantengo organizada. Cuando la situación se siente abrumadora, respiro y me reenfonco. Me comunico con mi equipo, porque el cuidado es un esfuerzo en conjunto."

💡 TIP
Show self-awareness and teamwork. Saying you never get stressed is not believable.
6
What is your availability? Can you work weekends or holidays?
¿Cuál es tu disponibilidad? ¿Puedes trabajar fines de semana o días festivos?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I am available [your specific days/shifts]. I understand that patient care does not stop on weekends or holidays, and I am willing to be flexible to meet the team needs when possible."

🇪🇸 Español

"Estoy disponible [tus días/turnos específicos]. Entiendo que el cuidado del paciente no se detiene los fines de semana, y estoy dispuesta a ser flexible cuando sea posible."

💡 TIP
Know your real availability before the interview. Honesty prevents future conflicts.
7
Are you comfortable with personal care — bathing, toileting, dressing?
¿Te sientes cómoda con el cuidado personal — baño, higiene íntima, vestir?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"Yes, absolutely. I understand that personal care is a fundamental and dignified part of this role. I always perform these tasks with professionalism, full respect, and attention to the patient privacy and comfort."

🇪🇸 Español

"Sí, absolutamente. Entiendo que el cuidado personal es fundamental. Siempre lo realizo con profesionalismo, respeto total y atención a la privacidad y comodidad del paciente."

💡 TIP
Confidence here is essential. Any hesitation may raise doubts about your readiness.
8
How do you ensure a patient's privacy and dignity?
¿Cómo aseguras la privacidad y dignidad del paciente?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I always knock before entering, explain what I will do before I do it, keep the patient covered during personal care, and never share patient information with unauthorized persons — always following HIPAA guidelines."

🇪🇸 Español

"Siempre toco antes de entrar, explico lo que voy a hacer, mantengo al paciente cubierto, y nunca comparto información del paciente con personas no autorizadas, siguiendo siempre HIPAA."

💡 TIP
Mentioning HIPAA shows you understand legal and ethical standards.
9
Are you comfortable with medication reminders?
¿Te sientes cómoda con recordatorios de medicamentos?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"Yes. As a CNA or HHA I do not administer medications, but I am responsible for reminding patients to take their prescribed medications on time and reporting any refusal or concern to the supervising nurse."

🇪🇸 Español

"Sí. Como CNA/HHA no administro medicamentos, pero soy responsable de recordarle al paciente tomarlos a tiempo y de reportar cualquier negativa a la enfermera supervisora."

💡 TIP
You REMIND — you do NOT administer. Knowing your scope is critical.
10
Do you have experience with patients with dementia or Alzheimer's?
¿Tienes experiencia con pacientes con demencia o Alzheimer's?
▼
🇺🇸 English

If yes: describe specific situations with empathy. If no: "I do not have direct clinical experience yet, but I understand it requires extra patience, consistent routines, calm communication, and strong emotional support. I am fully committed to learning."

🇪🇸 Español

Si no tienes: "Aún no tengo experiencia clínica directa, pero entiendo que requiere paciencia extra, rutinas consistentes y comunicación tranquila. Estoy totalmente comprometida a aprender."

💡 TIP
Honesty plus genuine willingness to learn is always valued over false claims.
11
Are you comfortable working with terminally ill patients?
¿Te sientes cómoda trabajando con pacientes en estado terminal?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"Yes. I understand it requires a special kind of compassion — providing comfort, dignity, and emotional support during an incredibly sensitive time. I am prepared to provide that care with full respect for the patient and their family."

🇪🇸 Español

"Sí. Entiendo que requiere compasión especial: brindar confort, dignidad y apoyo emocional. Estoy preparada para brindar ese cuidado con pleno respeto al paciente y su familia."

💡 TIP
Empathy and emotional maturity are the qualities to highlight here.
12
What do you know about infection control?
¿Qué sabes sobre control de infecciones?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"Infection control is essential to patient and staff safety. Key practices include frequent proper hand washing, correct use of PPE — gloves, masks, gowns — isolation precautions when needed, and proper disposal of contaminated materials."

🇪🇸 Español

"El control de infecciones es esencial. Las prácticas clave incluyen lavado de manos frecuente y adecuado, uso correcto de EPP — guantes, mascarillas, batas — y eliminación adecuada de materiales contaminados."

💡 TIP
Mention hand washing specifically — it is the single most important practice.
13
How do you prioritize multiple patients or tasks?
¿Cómo priorizas cuando tienes múltiples pacientes o tareas?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I assess urgency and patient needs first — safety always comes before convenience. I communicate with the nurse about priorities, organize tasks logically, and make sure no patient is left unattended."

🇪🇸 Español

"Evalúo primero la urgencia y las necesidades del paciente — la seguridad siempre es lo primero. Me comunico con la enfermera y me aseguro de que ningún paciente quede desatendido."

💡 TIP
Critical thinking + communication + calm under pressure — that is what they want to hear.
14
How do you communicate with patients' family members?
¿Cómo te comunicas con los familiares de los pacientes?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I communicate with kindness, honesty, and professionalism. I keep family informed about general well-being within HIPAA boundaries, listen to their concerns, and involve the nurse for any medical questions."

🇪🇸 Español

"Me comunico con amabilidad, honestidad y profesionalismo. Mantengo a los familiares informados dentro de los límites de HIPAA e involucro a la enfermera para preguntas médicas."

💡 TIP
You share general well-being updates — NOT medical decisions. HIPAA always applies.
15
Why should we hire you?
¿Por qué deberíamos contratarte?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"Because I bring not only the skills and knowledge required for this role, but also a genuine heart for caregiving. I am reliable, compassionate, a team player, and deeply committed to providing the best possible care to every patient I serve."

🇪🇸 Español

"Porque traigo no solo las habilidades necesarias, sino también un corazón genuino para el cuidado. Soy confiable, compasiva, trabajo en equipo y estoy profundamente comprometida a brindar la mejor atención posible."

💡 TIP
End with confidence and conviction. This is your closing argument — make every word count.
Situational / Scenarios · Preguntas Situacionales
16
A patient is crying and says they feel very lonely. What do you do?
Un paciente llora y dice que se siente muy solo. ¿Qué haces?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would stop what I am doing and give the patient my full attention. I would sit with them, listen without judgment, hold their hand if appropriate, and validate their feelings by saying something like: 'I hear you, and I am here with you right now.' I would then notify the nurse or social worker so the patient can receive additional emotional support."

🇪🇸 Español

"Me detendría y le daría toda mi atención. Me sentaría con ellos, escucharía sin juzgar, tomaría su mano si es apropiado, y validaría sus sentimientos. Luego notificaría a la enfermera o trabajadora social para que el paciente reciba apoyo adicional."

💡 TIP
Showing emotional intelligence here is key. Never dismiss a patient's feelings or rush away.
17
You notice a bruise on a patient that was not there before. What do you do?
Notas un moretón en un paciente que no estaba antes. ¿Qué haces?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would document the bruise objectively — location, size, color — and report it immediately to the nurse or supervisor. I would not ignore it or assume it is nothing. If abuse is suspected, I understand this is a mandatory reporting situation and I would follow all facility protocols without exception."

🇪🇸 Español

"Documentaría el moretón de forma objetiva — ubicación, tamaño, color — y lo reportaría de inmediato a la enfermera o supervisora. Nunca lo ignoraría. Si se sospecha abuso, seguiría todos los protocolos de la institución sin excepción."

💡 TIP
Objective documentation + immediate reporting = the correct and only answer here.
18
A family member asks you about the patient's diagnosis. How do you respond?
Un familiar te pregunta sobre el diagnóstico del paciente. ¿Cómo respondes?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would be kind but clear: 'I understand your concern and I want to help, but discussing a patient's medical diagnosis is outside my scope as a CNA/HHA. I will let the nurse know you have questions so she can speak with you directly.' I would then notify the nurse immediately."

🇪🇸 Español

"Sería amable pero clara: 'Entiendo su preocupación, pero hablar sobre el diagnóstico médico está fuera de mi alcance como CNA/HHA. Le informaré a la enfermera que tiene preguntas para que pueda hablar con usted directamente.' Luego notificaría a la enfermera de inmediato."

💡 TIP
Knowing and respecting your scope of practice protects you, the patient, and the family.
19
A home patient refuses to let you in when you arrive. What do you do?
Un paciente en el hogar se niega a dejarte entrar cuando llegas. ¿Qué haces?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would stay calm and not force entry. I would speak to the patient calmly through the door, try to understand their concern, and attempt to reassure them. If they continue to refuse, I would contact my supervisor immediately and document everything — the time, the patient's response, and all actions I took."

🇪🇸 Español

"Me mantendría calmada y no forzaría la entrada. Hablaría tranquilamente con el paciente a través de la puerta, intentaría entender su preocupación y tranquilizarlo. Si continúa negándose, contactaría a mi supervisora de inmediato y documentaría todo."

💡 TIP
Never force entry. Reporting and documenting protects both the patient and you legally.
20
A patient tells you they have not eaten in two days. What do you do?
Un paciente te dice que no ha comido en dos días. ¿Qué haces?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would take this seriously as it could indicate a medical or safety concern. I would report it to the nurse or supervisor immediately, document what the patient told me, and encourage the patient gently — without forcing — to eat or drink something if appropriate while waiting for the nurse's guidance."

🇪🇸 Español

"Lo tomaría en serio ya que podría indicar un problema médico o de seguridad. Lo reportaría a la enfermera o supervisora de inmediato, documentaría lo que el paciente me dijo, y alentaría gentilmente al paciente a comer o beber algo si es apropiado."

💡 TIP
Changes in eating are a red flag — always report, never assume it is not serious.
21
A patient becomes very agitated and starts yelling at you. What do you do?
Un paciente se vuelve muy agitado y comienza a gritarte. ¿Qué haces?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would remain calm and not take it personally — agitation is often a symptom of pain, fear, confusion, or medication. I would speak softly, maintain safe distance, try to de-escalate by validating their feelings, and notify the nurse if the situation does not improve or if I feel unsafe."

🇪🇸 Español

"Me mantendría calmada y no lo tomaría personal — la agitación suele ser un síntoma de dolor, miedo o confusión. Hablaría suavemente, mantendría distancia segura, intentaría de-escalar validando sus sentimientos, y notificaría a la enfermera si la situación no mejora."

💡 TIP
De-escalation, not confrontation. Your safety matters — involve the nurse when needed.
22
You are helping a patient walk and they suddenly feel dizzy. What do you do?
Estás ayudando a un paciente a caminar y de repente se siente mareado. ¿Qué haces?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would immediately stop and help the patient safely to a chair or lower them gently to the floor if there is no chair nearby. I would never let go of them. I would call for the nurse right away, keep the patient calm, and monitor them closely until help arrives. I would document the incident thoroughly."

🇪🇸 Español

"Me detendría de inmediato y ayudaría al paciente a sentarse seguramente o lo bajaría gentilmente al piso si no hay silla cerca. Nunca lo soltaría. Llamaría a la enfermera de inmediato, mantendría al paciente calmado y lo documentaría todo."

💡 TIP
Never let go of a dizzy patient. Safety first, call for help second.
23
What would you do if you made a mistake in patient care?
¿Qué harías si cometiste un error en el cuidado de un paciente?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would report it immediately to my supervisor — no matter how small it might seem. Patient safety comes first. I would document what happened honestly, and I would learn from the experience to prevent it from happening again."

🇪🇸 Español

"Lo reportaría de inmediato a mi supervisora, sin importar qué tan pequeño parezca. Documentaría lo ocurrido de manera honesta y aprendería de la experiencia para evitar que vuelva a ocurrir."

💡 TIP
Honesty, accountability, and reporting are non-negotiable in healthcare. This shows integrity.
24
A patient asks you to keep a secret from the nursing staff. What do you do?
Un paciente te pide que guardes un secreto del personal de enfermería. ¿Qué haces?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would listen to the patient with empathy, but explain clearly and kindly that I cannot keep secrets from the care team because my first responsibility is their safety and well-being. I would say: 'I care about you and I want to help, but I need to share anything that could affect your health with your nurse.' Then I would report it appropriately."

🇪🇸 Español

"Escucharía al paciente con empatía, pero explicaría claramente que no puedo guardar secretos del equipo de cuidado porque mi primera responsabilidad es su seguridad. Le diría: 'Me importas y quiero ayudarte, pero necesito compartir con tu enfermera cualquier cosa que pueda afectar tu salud.'"

💡 TIP
Patient safety always comes before keeping a secret. Transparency protects everyone.
25
You notice a patient's skin looks red over a bony area. What do you do?
Notas que la piel de un paciente se ve roja sobre un área ósea. ¿Qué haces?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would recognize this as a potential early stage pressure ulcer, also called a bedsore. I would immediately report it to the nurse without attempting to treat it myself. I would document the location, size, and appearance, and make sure the patient is repositioned to relieve pressure on that area."

🇪🇸 Español

"Reconocería esto como una posible úlcera por presión en etapa temprana. Lo reportaría de inmediato a la enfermera sin intentar tratarla yo misma. Documentaría la ubicación, tamaño y apariencia, y me aseguraría de reposicionar al paciente para aliviar la presión."

💡 TIP
Early detection of pressure ulcers saves lives. Report immediately — do not treat on your own.
Ethics & Values · Ética y Valores
26
What does patient dignity mean to you?
¿Qué significa para ti la dignidad del paciente?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"To me, dignity means treating every patient as a complete human being worthy of respect — regardless of their condition, age, or background. It means maintaining their privacy, speaking to them as an adult, honoring their choices when possible, and never making them feel ashamed or inferior for needing care."

🇪🇸 Español

"Para mí, dignidad significa tratar a cada paciente como un ser humano completo digno de respeto, sin importar su condición. Significa mantener su privacidad, hablarle como adulto, honrar sus decisiones cuando sea posible y nunca hacerle sentir vergüenza por necesitar cuidado."

💡 TIP
This answer reveals your character. Be genuine — interviewers will notice if it sounds rehearsed without feeling.
27
What would you do if you saw a coworker treat a patient disrespectfully?
¿Qué harías si vieras a un compañero tratar a un paciente de manera irrespetuosa?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would make sure the patient is safe first. Then I would report what I witnessed to my supervisor or charge nurse — calmly and factually, without personal accusations. Patient dignity and safety always come first, even if it means reporting a coworker. Staying silent is not an option when a patient is being harmed."

🇪🇸 Español

"Me aseguraría primero de que el paciente esté seguro. Luego reportaría lo que presencié a mi supervisora o enfermera a cargo, de forma calmada y objetiva. La dignidad y seguridad del paciente siempre son lo primero, aunque eso signifique reportar a un compañero."

💡 TIP
Employers need to know you will speak up. Silence in the face of abuse is never acceptable.
28
A patient or family member offers you money or a gift. What do you do?
Un paciente o familiar te ofrece dinero o un regalo. ¿Qué haces?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would decline politely and with gratitude. I would say something like: 'Thank you so much, that means a great deal to me, but it is not something I am allowed to accept. Providing you with the best care possible is my reward.' I would also inform my supervisor about the offer as required by most facility policies."

🇪🇸 Español

"Declinaría cortésmente y con gratitud, diciendo algo como: 'Muchas gracias, eso significa mucho para mí, pero no me está permitido aceptarlo. Brindarle la mejor atención posible es mi recompensa.' También informaría a mi supervisora según lo requieren las políticas de la institución."

💡 TIP
Most facilities prohibit accepting gifts. Knowing this policy shows professionalism.
29
How do you maintain professional boundaries with patients?
¿Cómo mantienes límites profesionales con los pacientes?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I maintain professional boundaries by keeping my personal life separate from my work, not sharing my personal contact information with patients, not engaging in personal financial transactions, and ensuring that all physical contact is purposeful and within the scope of care. I can be warm and caring while still being professional."

🇪🇸 Español

"Mantengo límites profesionales manteniendo mi vida personal separada del trabajo, no compartiendo mi información de contacto personal con pacientes, y asegurando que todo contacto físico sea con propósito de cuidado. Puedo ser cálida y cuidadosa mientras sigo siendo profesional."

💡 TIP
Professional boundaries protect both you and the patient. Warmth and professionalism are not opposites.
30
What would you do if a coworker asked you to falsify a patient record?
¿Qué harías si un compañero te pidiera falsificar un registro del paciente?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would refuse clearly and professionally. Falsifying documentation is illegal, dangerous, and a serious violation of medical ethics. I would report the request to my supervisor immediately. Accurate documentation is not optional — it directly affects patient safety, and I take that responsibility very seriously."

🇪🇸 Español

"Me negaría clara y profesionalmente. Falsificar documentación es ilegal, peligroso y una violación grave de la ética médica. Reportaría la solicitud a mi supervisora de inmediato. La documentación precisa no es opcional — afecta directamente la seguridad del paciente."

💡 TIP
Zero tolerance on this. It is illegal. The correct answer is: refuse and report.
Teamwork · Trabajo en Equipo
31
Describe a time you worked well as part of a team.
Describe una vez que trabajaste bien como parte de un equipo.
▼
🇺🇸 English

"Use the STAR method — Situation, Task, Action, Result. Example: 'When caring for a family member at home, I coordinated with visiting nurses and therapists. I shared observations clearly, followed their guidance, and made sure everyone was working toward the same goal: the patient's comfort and recovery. That experience showed me the power of clear communication in a care team.'"

🇪🇸 Español

"Usa el método STAR. Ej: 'Cuando cuidé a un familiar en casa, coordiné con enfermeras y terapeutas visitantes. Compartí observaciones claramente y me aseguré de que todos trabajáramos hacia el mismo objetivo: la comodidad y recuperación del paciente.'"

💡 TIP
Use a real example, even from personal caregiving experience. Specificity builds credibility.
32
How do you communicate effectively with nurses and supervisors?
¿Cómo te comunicas eficazmente con enfermeras y supervisores?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I communicate clearly, promptly, and professionally. I report any changes in a patient's condition as soon as I observe them. I ask questions when I am unsure — I never assume — and I follow the chain of command. I believe clear communication is the foundation of safe patient care."

🇪🇸 Español

"Me comunico de manera clara, rápida y profesional. Reporto cualquier cambio en la condición del paciente tan pronto como lo observo. Hago preguntas cuando no estoy segura — nunca asumo — y sigo la cadena de mando. Creo que la comunicación clara es la base del cuidado seguro."

💡 TIP
Mentioning the chain of command shows you understand structure and hierarchy in healthcare.
33
What do you do if you disagree with a coworker's approach to patient care?
¿Qué haces si no estás de acuerdo con el enfoque de un compañero hacia el cuidado del paciente?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"If it is not a safety issue, I would approach the coworker privately and respectfully share my concern. If it involves patient safety, I would escalate immediately to the nurse or supervisor — patient safety always comes first. I believe disagreements can be handled professionally without damaging team relationships."

🇪🇸 Español

"Si no es un problema de seguridad, abordaría al compañero en privado y compartiría mi preocupación respetuosamente. Si implica seguridad del paciente, escalaría de inmediato a la enfermera o supervisora. Creo que los desacuerdos se pueden manejar profesionalmente sin dañar las relaciones del equipo."

💡 TIP
Private first, supervisor second if safety is at risk. This shows maturity and professionalism.
34
How do you handle receiving criticism or feedback from a supervisor?
¿Cómo manejas recibir críticas o retroalimentación de un supervisor?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I welcome constructive feedback as an opportunity to grow. I listen without becoming defensive, ask clarifying questions if needed, thank the supervisor for the guidance, and implement the change right away. I understand that feedback is not personal — it is about improving care, and that is always the goal."

🇪🇸 Español

"Recibo la retroalimentación constructiva como una oportunidad de crecer. Escucho sin ponerme a la defensiva, hago preguntas si es necesario, agradezco la orientación e implemento el cambio de inmediato. Entiendo que la retroalimentación no es personal — es para mejorar el cuidado."

💡 TIP
Nobody wants to hire someone who cannot take feedback. Show openness and a growth mindset.
35
What would you do if a coworker was consistently not doing their job?
¿Qué harías si un compañero consistentemente no cumple con su trabajo?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would first focus on completing my own responsibilities and ensuring my patients are cared for. If the coworker's negligence was directly affecting patient safety, I would report it to the supervisor — not to cause trouble, but because patient welfare is the priority. I would document specific observations factually, not opinions."

🇪🇸 Español

"Primero me enfocaría en completar mis propias responsabilidades y asegurar que mis pacientes estén cuidados. Si la negligencia del compañero afecta directamente la seguridad del paciente, lo reportaría a la supervisora — no para causar problemas, sino porque el bienestar del paciente es la prioridad."

💡 TIP
Document facts, not opinions. Report for safety reasons, not personal conflicts.
Technical / Procedures · Preguntas Técnicas / Procedimientos
36
How do you safely reposition a bedridden patient?
¿Cómo reposicionas de forma segura a un paciente encamado?
▼
🇺🇸 English

"I would explain the procedure to the patient first. I use proper body mechanics — keeping my back straight, bending my knees, and using my legs for strength. I reposition at least every two hours to prevent pressure ulcers, use draw sheets or assistive devices when needed, and ensure the call light is within reach after repositioning."

🇪🇸 Español

"Primero explico el procedimiento al paciente. Uso mecánica corporal adecuada — espalda recta, rodillas dobladas, usando las piernas para el esfuerzo. Reposiciono al menos cada dos horas para prevenir úlceras por presión y me aseguro de que el timbre esté al alcance después."

💡 TIP
Every 2 hours is the standard. Proper body mechanics protects both the patient and you.
37
Describe the proper steps for hand washing.
Describe los pasos correctos para el lavado de manos.
▼
🇺🇸 English

"Wet hands with clean running water. Apply soap. Lather for at least 20 seconds — scrub all surfaces including backs of hands, between fingers, and under nails. Rinse thoroughly under running water. Dry with a clean towel and use the towel to turn off the faucet. Hand washing is the single most effective way to prevent the spread of infection."

🇪🇸 Español

Interview Tips · Consejos para la Entrevista

Prepare well, present yourself with confidence · Prepárate bien y preséntate con confianza

1

Arrive 10 minutes early

Punctuality signals reliability and respect for the interviewer time.

La puntualidad demuestra responsabilidad y respeto.

2

Dress professionally — clean scrubs

Clean, wrinkle-free scrubs show you understand the healthcare environment.

Uniformes limpios demuestran que entiendes el entorno de salud.

3

Use the STAR method

Situation – Task – Action – Result. Structure your answers around real examples from your life.

Situación – Tarea – Acción – Resultado. Usa ejemplos reales.

4

Maintain eye contact

Looking at the interviewer communicates confidence and honesty — both critical for caregiving roles.

Mirar al entrevistador demuestra confianza y honestidad.

5

Prepare 2–3 questions to ask

Ask about the team, training schedule, or what a typical shift looks like. Shows genuine interest.

Pregunta sobre el equipo o la capacitación. Demuestra interés genuino.

6

Take your time before answering

A brief pause shows thoughtfulness — not weakness. Breathe, organize your thoughts, then respond.

Una pausa breve muestra reflexión, no debilidad. Respira y responde con claridad.

7

Bring documents ready

Bring your CNA/HHA certification, ID, references, and training certificates. Being prepared impresses.

Trae tu certificación, ID, referencias y certificados. Estar preparada impresiona.

8

Close with a power phrase

"I am very excited about this opportunity and I am confident I would be a great fit for your team and your patients."

"Estoy muy emocionada por esta oportunidad y segura de ser una excelente adición para su equipo."
